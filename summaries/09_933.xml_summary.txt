First, the reference in the definition in s.3(1) to "a decision of an administrative character made ... under an enactment" indicates that a reviewable decision is a decision which a statute requires or authorizes rather than merely a step taken in the course of reasoning on the way to the making of the ultimate decision. COMPLAINT  The applicant seeks to review the respondent's decision to issue a payment demand on 27 February 2008 against the applicant's interests. The relevant policy considerations are competing. The decisions lacked any quality of finality and were not substantive determinations.
