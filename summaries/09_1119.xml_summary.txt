It is agreed that the actions of the second respondent, and the first respondent vicariously, in respect of the stop-work meeting constituted building industrial action within the meaning of s 36 of the BCII Act. Both contraventions are relatively minor. The refusal of the second respondent to induct them did not cause any significant loss of time or significant delay on the project. The phrase "undue pressure" is not defined in the BCII Act. Accountability for unlawful conduct is always the object of the penalty process.
