It follows from these principles that s 340(2) should be construed by reference to its context, language and purpose. An employee who is given false and misleading information that may affect his or her decision on whether to approve an agreement is, by the very provision of that information, denied a reasonable opportunity to decide whether to approve the agreement. I therefore consider that Blue Star used a process which ensured that eligible employees, and only eligible employees, cast the votes in the postal ballot. At the time, there were four employees employed at the ICB Central site.
