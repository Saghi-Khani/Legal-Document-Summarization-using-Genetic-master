The oddity in Chanel's position is that s 137(5) appears to impose a duty on the Customs CEO to release seized goods if, after the expiration of the 20 day period, there is no court order in force preventing their release. Section 131 provides that the purpose of Part 13 is the protection of registered trade marks by making provision allowing the Customs CEO to seize and deal with infringing goods. Any other construction, so it contends, would render the phrase superfluous.
