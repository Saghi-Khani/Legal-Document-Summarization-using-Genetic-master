The circumstance that the legitimate claim of the debtor is one against the judgment creditor is likely to be a significant circumstance for the purposes of s 52(2)(b). There is no substance to that argument essentially because the costs order supporting the bankruptcy notice and the creditor's petition was made in the proceedings instituted by Mr Totev in the District Court, which are the very proceedings which, he says, constitute his counter-claim, set off or cross-demand. It plainly was not a matter which amounted to "other sufficient cause".
