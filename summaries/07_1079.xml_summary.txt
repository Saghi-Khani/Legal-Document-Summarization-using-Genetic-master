The following are relevant considerations in the context of whether the remission discretion should be exercised:  • the amount of the tax shortfall • that the Act imposed a penalty at the rate of 50% • that the whole of the shortfall amount resulted from recklessness as to the operation of a taxation law • any circumstances peculiar to the taxpayer. The Tribunal said: "Section 298- 20 of Schedule 1 to the TA Act provides that the Commissioner may remit all or part of a penalty. The Commissioner does not impose the penalty, his discretion is limited to remitting the penalty.
