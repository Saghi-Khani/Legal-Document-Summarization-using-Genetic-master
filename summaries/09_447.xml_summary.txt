It is implicit in O 72 r 7(1)(b)(ii) that an agreement reached at a mediation conference can only be the subject of an order or a judgment if the parties consent. If the respondents then decline to execute the deed the matter can be reviewed but I would have thought at that stage the applicant would be in a much stronger position to move for judgment. ...  It has always been the case that a court has jurisdiction on motion in the action to enforce terms of settlement. The applicant was then represented by different solicitors.
