Notwithstanding considerable reservation, it is considered that leave to appeal should be refused. It is not for this Court to provide the findings and reasons that should have been provided by the Federal Magistrate. Also relevant to any exercise of discretion is any explanation for the failure to raise the fresh argument sought to be relied upon: e.g. He accepted that the argument set forth in his written Outline of Submissions had not been advanced before the Federal Magistrate. The Tribunal used this information while making the decision. The applicant's evidence was vague and full of generalities.
