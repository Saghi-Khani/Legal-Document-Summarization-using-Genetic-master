I find that each Message was reasonably likely, in all the circumstances, to offend and insult the applicant or (if it be necessary) other persons of Jewish race or ethnicity. It contends that, as owner and manager of the website, it merely provided a facility --- the Forum --- by which the second respondent published the Messages. The offensive parts of the Messages are quite explicit. The case against the party posting the publications is straightforward. Silberberg v The Builders Collective of Australia Inc [2007] FCA 1512 (2 October 2007) "Phil" is obviously Dwyer. Such accepted responsibility could be established by inference.
