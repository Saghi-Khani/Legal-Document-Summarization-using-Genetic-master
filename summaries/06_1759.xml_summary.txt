The applicant submitted that the Tribunal had not given consideration to the definition of torture in Art 7(2)(e) and had erred in failing to make a finding about whether the infliction of severe pain or suffering had been intentional. It did so. This ground concerns any defence of superior orders which may have been available as a matter of customary international law at the time the alleged crimes were committed by the applicant notwithstanding that under the Rome Statute, no such defence is, in effect, available in relation to crimes against humanity.
