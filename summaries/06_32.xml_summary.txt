That Act provided that the only person who could apply for a patent was the "actual inventor" or his assigns or legal representatives (if deceased) or a person to whom the invention had been communicated by the "actual inventor" or his representatives or assigns: s 7(3). The first is Gibbons v Ogden , 22 US 1 (1824). This was changed by the Patents, Designs, and Trade Marks Act 1883 , 46 & 47 Vict, c 57, ss 4 and 5.
